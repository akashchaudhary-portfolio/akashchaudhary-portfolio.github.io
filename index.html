Akash Chaudhary

UX Researcher
Authentic User Experience Lab, University of California, Santa Cruz

Hi there! I think human behaviors are intriguing. We are thoughtful but at times also subject to our cultural biases and individual preferences. Tech "disruptions" make our behavior vulnerable to easy change, but at times these behaviors might be worth "preserving". That's why I like exploring human values and motivations around the use of technology in our daily habitual activities. My projects explore themes like human language, psychology, emotions, digital wellbeing and in general exploring online behaviors, consequently designing interfaces for enabling and affording better emotional expressions in the day to day lives of people.



Current project

I am currently exploring the experiences of people living in rural areas. We realized that many people like living in rural areas of California as they relish the sense of quiet and calm solitude, natural beauty, extended personal space, and care and warmth offerend in the close-knit communities. These people usually try to find cheap rural areas not too far from the San Francisco bay area where they can travel and work when required. This sort of personal and working symbiosis exposes people to certain gaps in infrastructure facilities, like computing and transportation in and around rural areas. We intend to explore some of these shortcomings in the rural infrastructure by designing an interactive artifact that engages with people in meaningful ways.



Prior projects

In my previous projects, I analyzed user habits on video streaming platforms, specifically exploring compulsive online behaviors. The project revealed the role UI design elements that act as user impediments to digital wellbeing on online platforms. I also realized that English speaking habits in non-native speakers is flawed and problematic. Subsequently I designed and tested the performance of a speech improvement mobile application through iterative design phases. I am currently seeking job opportunities as a UX researcher.

Published Projects

DISCOVERING UNINTENDED USER BEHAVIORS DUE TO DARK UI PATTERNS

  • Overwatching online user behaviors on video streaming platforms is a problem.
  • We carried out a diary study on 22 viewers across 228 viewing sessions to gain insights into user feelings and state of minds while they video watch.
  • We expose problematic user watching trends, and recognize dark UI patterns prevalent across video streaming platforms.
  • Our study highlights 4 ethically oriented UI design considerations to safeguard user interests on current video streaming platforms.

VERBOSE : A VOICE-INPUT APP FOR SPEECH IMPROVEMENT

  • People with English as seond language speak in a way that is difficult to understand.
  • We carried out 4 iterative user testing design stages using --- cue card study, low-fidelity wireframe prototype testing, audio-visual surveys and high-fidelity mobile app testing.
  • This iterative testing provides 8 essential design requirements that are necessary to develop a speech intonation mobile application for non-native speakers.
  • The final mobile application helped people in improving the intent of their speech expressions.

Class Projects

DESIGNING FOR EMOTION REGULATION IN DOGS

  • Dogs feel separation anxiety when isolated from owners.
  • I conducted 3 interviews with dog owners for exploring their interaction patterns with their dogs.
  • We came up with 4 non-anthropomorphic design solutions for an embodied tool that can help regulate dog emotions when not with their owners.
  • Our interviews provide initial proof for 4 design classifications that help explore the design space of an embodied tool which can be utilized for designing dog toys.

DATA SONIFICATION

  • Artists face a problem in having a common cross-modal media transfer unit that can help communicate and edit MIDI messages across different softwares.
  • We designed a python-based toolbox with a Kivy GUI for converting, editing and navigating MIDI messages across different softwares, like Reaper and SuperCollider.
  • Our tool has implications in acting as a bridging backbone for creative work across different platforms.
